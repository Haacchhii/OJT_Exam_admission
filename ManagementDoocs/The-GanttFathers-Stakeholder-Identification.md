## 1. Introduction

The purpose of identifying stakeholders for the GKISSJ project — the Online Entrance Exams & Admissions System for Golden Key Integrated School of St. Joseph — is to ensure effective collaboration, clear communication, and alignment of interests among all involved parties. By understanding the roles, influence, and needs of each stakeholder, the project can optimize engagement strategies, mitigate risks, and enhance overall success.

## 3. Stakeholder Analysis

|  | Parents / Guardians | |
|  | Teachers / Exam Coordinators | |

### Analysis Notes

- **Manage Closely (High Influence, High Interest):** School Principal, Registrar's Office, and Project Manager are the most critical stakeholders. They drive project decisions and are directly impacted by the system's success or failure.
- **Keep Satisfied (High Influence, Low Interest):** NPC and Supabase have significant influence on compliance and infrastructure but are not actively involved in day-to-day decisions.
- **Keep Informed (Low Influence, High Interest):** Students, parents, and teachers are end users who are highly interested in the system but have limited influence on project direction. Their feedback is crucial for usability.
- **Monitor (Low Influence, Low Interest):** DepEd has indirect influence through regulatory standards but is not actively involved with individual school systems.
